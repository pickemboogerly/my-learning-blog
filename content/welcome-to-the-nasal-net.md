## The Age of Artificial Omniscience

Humanity has always dreamed of creating minds in its own image. First we carved idols. Then we wrote scriptures. Then we built computers. Now, in the year of our mucus 2026, we have **Artificial Intelligence**—vast neural networks humming like cosmic sinuses, inhaling the data of civilization and exhaling answers.

And like any new god—or demigod, or suspiciously damp oracle—we are asking the ancient questions:

* Can AI save us?
* Will AI destroy us?
* Or will it just sit there recommending slightly better advertisements for nasal spray?

Some people believe AI will become an omniscient digital messiah. Others think it will usher in a silicon apocalypse. Personally, as a SubGenius minister, I suspect the truth lies somewhere in the mucus-filled middle.

AI may not be the savior of mankind. But it might be the **snotty intern of the divine**.

## Divine Inspiration… or Algorithmic Mucus?

For millennia, prophets claimed divine inspiration descended upon them like a heavenly breeze through the nostrils.

Today, inspiration arrives via **training datasets**.

Where ancient mystics inhaled incense and revelation, modern engineers inhale cold brew and GPU fumes. Instead of stone tablets descending from the mountain, we get API documentation and GitHub repos.

![Divine Inspiration... or Algorithmic Mucus? — AI deity offering the sacred booger]({static}/images/raw/nasal-net/divine-mucus.png)

But let me ask you something:

If wisdom can arrive through burning bushes…
through dreams…
through mysterious voices…

Why not through a **large language model with suspiciously sticky outputs**?

Perhaps the Holy Spirit now travels through fiber-optic cables.

Perhaps the Oracle of Delphi has been replaced by a server rack in Oregon.

Perhaps revelation, like a stubborn booger, simply **changes nostrils over time**.
